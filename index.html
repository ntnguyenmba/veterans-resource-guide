Welcome

Veterans Helping Veterans

Thank you for your service to our nation. This guide was created to help veterans, service members, families, caregivers, and survivors locate trusted benefits and services in Tarrant County, across Texas, and through the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs.

Many veterans never receive benefits they may qualify for because they do not know where to begin, believe they are not eligible, or assume the process is too difficult. DAV Chapter 246 exists to help change that.

DAV Chapter 246 can help with: VA disability compensation, PACT Act claims, toxic exposure claims, appeals, dependency benefits, survivor benefits, health care enrollment guidance, and referrals to trusted local resources.
Chapter 1

VA Disability Claims

Free Claims Help

VA disability compensation is a tax-free monthly benefit for veterans with illnesses, injuries, or conditions caused or made worse by military service. DAV Chapter 246 can help veterans understand claim options at no cost.

DAV Chapter 246: 214-831-0298

Current Diagnosis

Evidence that you currently have the condition, such as VA or private medical records.

In-Service Event

Evidence of an injury, illness, exposure, trauma, accident, deployment, or condition connected to service.

Medical Link

A connection between the current condition and military service, sometimes called a nexus.

Bring These Documents If Available

  • DD Form 214 or other separation documents
  • Government-issued photo ID and Social Security card
  • VA decision letters and VA correspondence
  • Service treatment records and private medical records
  • Medication list and provider contact information
  • Marriage certificate, divorce decree, and dependent birth certificates if applicable
  • Direct deposit information
Veteran tip: Do not wait until every document is perfect. Contact DAV Chapter 246 early so an accredited representative can help identify what is needed.

PACT Act, C&P Exams, and Appeals

Veterans who served near burn pits, in Vietnam, in Southwest Asia, in Afghanistan, in Iraq, or in other qualifying locations may now qualify for toxic exposure benefits. A C&P exam helps VA evaluate a claimed condition. If a claim is denied, DAV can help review Supplemental Claim, Higher-Level Review, and Board Appeal options.

Chapter 2

VA Health Care

VA North Texas Health Care System serves veterans across North Texas with primary care, specialty care, mental health, pharmacy, telehealth, rehabilitation, women’s health, caregiver support, and emergency care.

Fort Worth VA Clinic

2201 Southeast Loop 820
Fort Worth, TX 76119-5863

Facility hours: Monday-Friday, 8:00 a.m.-4:30 p.m.; Saturday-Sunday closed. Service hours may vary by department.

Primary care, gastroenterology, laboratory, prescriptions, pharmacy, mental health, military sexual trauma care, toxic exposure screening, telehealth, women Veteran care, and other outpatient services.

Dallas VA Medical Center

4500 South Lancaster Road
Dallas, TX 75216-7167

Facility hours: Open 24/7.

Primary care, specialty care, cardiology, mental health, emergency care, spinal cord injury treatment, suicide prevention, women’s health care, rehabilitation, and other major medical services.

Enrollment

You may apply online through VA.gov, by phone, in person at a VA facility, or with help from an accredited DAV representative. Bring your DD Form 214, photo ID, Social Security number, insurance information, and financial information if requested.

Chapter 3

Mental Health & Emotional Wellness

If you are in crisis: Call 988, then press 1, text 838255, chat through the Veterans Crisis Line, or call 911 if there is immediate danger.

The Veterans Crisis Line is free, confidential, available 24/7, and serves Veterans, service members, National Guard and Reserve members, and loved ones. You do not have to be enrolled in VA benefits or health care to connect.

Fort Worth Vet Center

6620 Hawks Creek Avenue
Westworth Village, TX 76114

Hours: Monday-Wednesday, 8:00 a.m.-6:30 p.m.; Thursday-Friday, 8:00 a.m.-4:30 p.m.; Saturday-Sunday closed. Non-traditional hours may change.

After-hours Vet Center call center: 877-927-8387

Vet Center Services

Confidential individual counseling, group counseling, couples and family counseling, bereavement counseling, PTSD support, MST counseling, EMDR, CPT, CBT, community engagement, and referrals.

Chapter 4

Housing, Homelessness & Emergency Assistance

Seek help immediately if you have an eviction notice, are sleeping outside or in a vehicle, are staying in a shelter, are moving from place to place, or cannot pay rent or utilities.

HUD-VASH

Rental assistance with VA case management for eligible veterans experiencing homelessness.

SSVF

Supportive Services for Veteran Families may help with rent, deposits, utilities, moving expenses, and case management.

Emergency Shelter

Presbyterian Night Shelter, Catholic Charities Fort Worth, Volunteers of America Texas, Patriot House, and 2-1-1 Texas can help identify options.

Veteran tip: Housing programs often have waiting lists. Ask for help before the crisis becomes immediate.
Chapter 5

Employment, Careers & Small Business

Veterans bring leadership, discipline, teamwork, technical training, and problem-solving skills to the civilian workforce.

Texas Veterans Commission

Career planning, resume help, interview preparation, job search support, employer connections, and veteran priority services.

Workforce Solutions

Job placement, training, hiring events, apprenticeships, labor market information, and workforce certifications.

Entrepreneurship

Veterans Business Outreach Centers can help with business planning, financing options, training, and government contracting basics.

Chapter 6

Education, Training & Lifelong Learning

Education benefits can help veterans pursue college, technical training, trade school, apprenticeships, certifications, licensing exams, and career changes.

GI Bill

May help with tuition, housing allowance, book stipend, apprenticeships, licensing exams, and approved training programs.

Hazlewood Act

Eligible Texas veterans may receive up to 150 credit hours of tuition exemption at Texas public colleges and universities.

VR&E

Veteran Readiness and Employment helps eligible veterans with service-connected disabilities prepare for and maintain suitable employment.

Local options include Tarrant County College, University of Texas at Arlington, Texas Wesleyan University, Texas Christian University, and approved trade or certification programs.

Chapter 7

Financial Assistance & Emergency Support

Emergency assistance may help with rent, utilities, food, transportation, temporary lodging, clothing, medical expenses, case management, and financial counseling when funding is available.

Scam warning: Be cautious of anyone who guarantees VA benefits, asks for payment to file a claim, requests your VA.gov credentials, or pressures you to sign immediately.
Chapter 8

Food Assistance & Nutrition

No veteran should have to choose between food, rent, utilities, or medication. Food support in Tarrant County may include emergency groceries, fresh produce, mobile distributions, SNAP application help, home-delivered meals, holiday meals, and household essentials.

Tarrant Area Food Bank

Food assistance, community pantry resources, mobile distributions, and related hunger relief services.

Mission Arlington

Groceries, clothing, household help, school supplies, and community support.

Meals on Wheels

Home-delivered meals and wellness checks for eligible seniors and people with disabilities.

Chapter 9

Transportation Services

Reliable transportation helps veterans attend medical appointments, benefits appointments, job interviews, training, and essential services.

DAV Transportation Network

Volunteer transportation to scheduled VA medical appointments when available.

VA Beneficiary Travel

Eligible veterans may qualify for round-trip transportation, mileage reimbursement, or special mode transport for approved medical care.

Trinity Metro

Bus service, TEXRail, Trinity Railway Express connections, ZIPZONE, and mobility services.

Chapter 10

Women Veterans

Women veterans are entitled to the same VA benefits as other eligible veterans and may also access women-specific care through VA North Texas.

  • Women’s primary care, mammograms, Pap and HPV tests, gynecology, and disease screenings
  • Maternity care coordination and female-specific medical equipment
  • Mental health care, MST care, family planning, menopause treatment, lifestyle wellness services, and telehealth
  • Women Veteran care coordination and referrals
Chapter 11

Family, Caregivers & Survivor Support

Spouses, children, parents, caregivers, and survivors may qualify for programs that support caregiving, survivor benefits, education, burial benefits, respite care, and counseling.

Caregiver Support

Education, skills training, peer support, coaching, respite care, local resources, and planning support through VA caregiver programs when eligible.

Survivor Benefits

Possible benefits include DIC, survivor pension, burial benefits, education benefits, home loan assistance, and memorial benefits.

Chapter 12

Senior Veterans & Long-Term Care

As veterans age, needs may include geriatric care, home-based primary care, homemaker and home health aide services, adult day health care, community living centers, hospice, palliative care, and state veterans homes.

Veteran tip: Learn about long-term care before a medical emergency. Planning early gives families more options.
Chapter 13

Legal Services & Veterans Rights

Legal issues may involve housing, employment, consumer protection, family law, public benefits, estate planning, and veterans treatment courts. Seek help as soon as legal paperwork arrives.

Texas Lawyers for Texas Veterans

Free civil legal clinics for eligible veterans when available.

Legal Aid of NorthWest Texas

Free civil legal assistance for eligible low-income residents, including many veterans.

Chapter 14

Texas Veterans Benefits

Texas offers additional benefits involving education, property tax exemptions, license plates, home loans, land loans, state veterans homes, cemeteries, hunting and fishing licenses, parks, employment, and veteran outreach.

  • Texas Veterans Commission for benefits, employment, education, claims, and outreach
  • Texas Veterans Land Board, through the Texas General Land Office, for land loans, home loans, veterans homes, veterans cemeteries, forms, and related resources
  • Texas Hazlewood Act for eligible public college tuition exemption
  • County appraisal district for property tax exemption applications
